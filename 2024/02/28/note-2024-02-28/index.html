Circuit topology

Two or more element are in series if they are cascaded or connected sequentially and consequently carry the same current.

Two or more element are in parallel if they are connected to the same two nodes and consequently have the same voltage across them.

(2)Ohm’s Law, KCL(Kirchhoff’s Current Law) and KVL(Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law)

Kirchhoff’s First Law is based on the Law of conservation of charge, which requires that the algebraic sum of charge within a system cannot change.

KCL:

the algebraic sum of currents entering a node (or a close boundary) is zero.

Mathematical:

$$
KCL: \sum^N_{n=1}i_n=0
$$

N —— the number of branches connected to the node.

in —— the n(th) current entering the node.

currents entering the node may be regard as positive, which leaving the node may be regard as negative or vice versa.
$$
i_1-i_2+i_3+i_4-i_5=0 \rightarrow i_1+i_3+i_4 = i_2+i_5
$$
The sum of the currents entering a node is equal to

the sum of the currents leaving the node.
